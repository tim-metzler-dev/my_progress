## Today I learned:
	1. The iframe tag. It's like a window for an html document (maybe google maps or youtube) that isn't yours but you want to use it in your side.
		a. <iframe width="…" height="…" title="…" src="…" id="…" allowfullscreen (
		  attribute allows the user to display the iframe in full screen mode)>
		  If the maker did an headline you don't like you can't change it because it's not in your website.
		  You can use srcdoc instead of src to embed direct html
		b. Allow="
	  	accelerometer (the thing that notices when you turn your phone around)
		  autoplay (starts playing the video automaticly)
		  Clipboard-write (allows the iframe to copy the url in your clipboard)
	2. The embed tag. You use it for external things that aren't  html codes like pdf etc. 
	3. The <br /> tag makes a line break
	4. The <hr /> tag makes an horizontal rule
	5. The <small></small> tag makes the text in it smaller. Often used for licenses.
